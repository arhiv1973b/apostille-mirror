**SUBJECT:** Systemic Impunity and Criminal Identity Fraud in the Republic of Moldova.
**URGENCY:** HIGH (Hearing scheduled for April 9, 2026).

## 🚩 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This case represents a critical failure of the rule of law, combining a 24-year concealment of torture (1998-2021) with active identity fraud committed by politically connected actors.

## ⚖️ KEY FINDINGS
1. **Jus Cogens Breach:** The state concealed the 1998 torture evidence until Feb 11, 2021 (Apostille verification), creating a "procedural vacuum" of 8,158 days.
2. **Identity Fraud:** Liudmila Nantoi and accomplice Oazu Nantoi used the identity of the late I.M. Marcov to falsify documents and seize inheritance assets belonging to a Person with Disability (A©tor).
3. **Set-off Protocol:** A legal set-off (Art. 967 CC RM) was registered on March 19, 2026, to liquidate state claims based on the unpaid debt for human rights violations.

## 🌐 INTERNATIONAL IMPLICATIONS
The upcoming hearing on April 9 is a test for Moldova's judicial integrity. Failure to apply Jus Cogens norms signals a collapse of international legal obligations (Erga Omnes).
