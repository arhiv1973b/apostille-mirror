## LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### 1. Hague Convention of 5 October 1961

The Apostille Convention establishes an international procedure to authenticate public documents:

> **Article 1**: The apostille certifies **the authenticity of the signature**, the capacity in which the person signing the document has acted, and the identity of the seal or stamp.

**CRITICAL ISSUE**: An apostille with "unclear signature" **fails the fundamental purpose** of the Convention. The very document that guarantees authenticity instead certifies uncertainty.

### 2. Jus Cogens: Prohibition of Torture

- The prohibition of torture is recognized as a **jus cogens norm** (imperative international law)
- No derogation is permitted under any circumstances
- States have an absolute obligation to prevent, investigate, and punish torture

### 3. Erga Omnes Obligations

- The obligation to investigate torture is of **erga omnes character**
- This duty is owed to the **international community as a whole**
- All signatory states have a legal interest in ensuring compliance

## THE KEY LEGAL QUESTION

**If all signatory states of the Hague Convention leave this system intact—where apostilles certify documents with unverifiable signatures—are they:**

1. **Complicity in Institutional Impunity** — By allowing a certification mechanism to be used for documents that cannot be verified, states become complicit in obscuring potential forgeries

2. **Violation of Erga Omnes** — The duty to investigate and ensure justice for torture victims extends to ensuring that certification mechanisms are not weaponized against victims

3. **Failure to Protect Victims** — The interests of torture victims are directly harmed when the very documents used against them bear unverifiable authentications

## CONCLUSION

The presence of 13+ apostilles with "unclear signatures" in a single case registry is not an anomaly—it is evidence of a systemic failure or deliberate obfuscation.

**For Torture Victims:**

When the documents used against you bear certifications that cannot be verified, you are denied the ability to challenge the authenticity of the very instruments used to perpetuate injustice.

If signatory states allow this system to persist without correction, they become parties to the institutional protection of impunity.
