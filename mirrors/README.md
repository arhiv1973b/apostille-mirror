# LEGAL CASE: APOSTILLE DAMAGE CLAIM — MACERET v. REPUBLIC OF MOLDOVA

## Case Reference: Apostille-Claim-2026

**Plaintiff:** Maceret Alexei Artur (A©tor)
**Claim Amount:** €500,000
**Period:** 1997–2026 (29 years)
**Apostilles Processed:** 97 documents (2021–2023)

---

## Summary

This repository documents a civil lawsuit against the Republic of Moldova for damages arising from:

- Systematic judicial persecution of a torture victim over 29 years
- Deliberate concealment of the Plaintiff's victim status (Art. 3 ECHR)
- Conversion of legitimately paid apostille services into defective documents
- Violation of Art. 5 of the Hague Convention 1961 through deliberate omission of signatory identification

### Core Legal Issue: Defective Apostilles as Institutional Sabotage

13 of 90 apostilles (14.4%) were rendered defective through deliberate omission of the **printed signatory name** — a mandatory field under Art. 5 of the Hague Convention 1961. The signature physically exists; what was omitted is the legible identification of the responsible official. This is not administrative error — it is a pattern of procedural sabotage against a non-rehabilitated torture victim whose status was actively concealed by state authorities.

1. ECHR: Art. 3 (torture), Art. 6 (fair trial), Art. 13 (effective remedy)
2. Hague Convention 1961 — Art. 5 (mandatory signatory identification)
3. Civil Code of Moldova — Art. 1386, 1387, 1401
4. Rome Statute of the ICC — Art. 7, 17, 29
5. Jus Cogens / Erga Omnes — absolute obligation to investigate and remedy torture
