# APOSTILLE DEFECTS REGISTER
## Field 2 Violations — Art. 5 Hague Convention 1961

---

## Legal Basis

**Art. 5, Hague Convention 1961** requires that apostille **Field 2** contain:
> *"the name and surname or the title of the person who has signed the public document"*

**Field 7** identifies the head of the apostille authority (certified via e-signature/digital certificate).

**The violation:** Field 2 was left blank or filled with *"semnatura indescifrabila"* (illegible signature) — meaning the physical signature exists, but the **printed identification of the signatory was deliberately omitted**.

This makes independent verification (graphological comparison against specimen signatures at the Ministry of Justice) **impossible** — which is the legal effect sought by the omission.

## Legal Consequence

Under Art. 5 Hague Convention, an apostille without legible signatory identification in Field 2:
1. **Cannot be verified** via graphological comparison
2. **Has no legal force abroad** — the certifying authority cannot be identified
3. Constitutes **defective performance** of a paid-for public service
4. May constitute **procedural fraud** where the defect is systematic and the victim status of the document holder was known to state authorities
