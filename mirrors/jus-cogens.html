⚡ Jus Cogens

Peremptory Norms of General International Law

Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties, 23 May 1969

What is Jus Cogens?

A peremptory norm of general international law (jus cogens) is a norm accepted and recognized by the international community of states as a whole as a norm from which no derogation is permitted and which can be modified only by a subsequent norm of general international law having the same character.

Unlike ordinary treaty obligations, jus cogens norms bind all states regardless of consent, reservations, or domestic law. Any act, treaty, or governmental decision that conflicts with jus cogens is void ab initio.

Vienna Convention — Key Articles

Article 53 — Void Treaties

"A treaty is void if, at the time of its conclusion, it conflicts with a peremptory norm of general international law. For the purposes of the present Convention, a peremptory norm of general international law is a norm accepted and recognized by the international community of States as a whole as a norm from which no derogation is permitted..."

Article 64 — Emergence of New Norms

"If a new peremptory norm of general international law emerges, any existing treaty which is in conflict with that norm becomes void and terminates."

Article 85 — Languages of Authenticity

The Convention was concluded in Chinese, English, French, Russian and Spanish, each text being equally authentic — establishing multilingual equal authentication as a norm. Moldova's refusal on language grounds violates this principle.

Application to Moldova Case

The Republic of Moldova, as signatory to the Vienna Convention (1969), Hague Convention (1961), and European Convention on Human Rights, is bound by jus cogens norms. The systematic processing of 90 apostilles (1997–2026) with authentication defects, combined with the refusal of Constitutional Court access on language grounds, constitutes:

  • Violation of Art. 53 — administrative acts conflicting with peremptory norms are void
  • Violation of Art. 64 — emerging authentication standards render non-compliant apostilles void
  • Violation of ECHR Art. 3, 6, 13 — systematic, not isolated breaches
  • Breach of Hague Convention Art. 3 — 14.4% unclear signatures

Total claim: €500,000 for period 1997–2026.
