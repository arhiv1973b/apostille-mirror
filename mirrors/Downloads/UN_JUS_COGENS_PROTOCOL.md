## IV. LEGAL ARGUMENTATION

### A. Jus Cogens Qualification

The prohibition of torture is recognized as **jus cogens** - an imperative norm of international law:

> "Any treaty that conflicts with a jus cogens norm is void ab initio"  
> (Vienna Convention Art. 53)

**Application:** The 1998 torture generated a "poisoned fruit" effect. All derivative proceedings (2003-2025) are void as they are based on evidence obtained through torture.

### B. Continuing Breach

The Moldovan courts' decisions of December 2025 (Judges Stambol, Holban) blocking rehabilitation constitute:

- **Continuing international wrong**
- **Active obstruction of jus cogens enforcement**
- **Loss of procedural privileges** under Vienna Convention Art. 60

### C. Erga Omnes Character

> "When obligations erga omnes are at stake, all States have a legal interest in their observance"  
> (ICJ, Barcelona Traction, 1970)

**Consequence:** The violation affects the international legal order as a whole, not just the individual victim.
