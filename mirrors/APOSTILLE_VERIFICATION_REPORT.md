## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This report documents the technical and legal verification of 90 apostille documents issued by Moldovan government institutions. The investigation revealed **13 fraudulent apostilles (14.4%)** that violate Article 5 of the Hague Convention.

### Key Findings:

- **Total Documents Verified:** 90 apostilles
- **Fraudulent/Void:** 13 documents
- **Verified Authentic:** 77 documents
- **Critical Violation:** Empty Field 3 (no surname/position of official)

### 1.2 Verification Criteria

Per Article 5 of the Hague Convention, each apostille MUST contain:

- [ ] Field 1: Country
- [ ] Field 2: Signature of signing authority
- [ ] **Field 3: Surname/Position of signing official** (CRITICAL)
- [ ] Field 4: Name of agency
- [ ] Field 5: Date
- [ ] Field 6: Issuing authority
- [ ] Field 7: Digital/seal signature
- [ ] Field 8: Registration number

## 2. VIOLATION ANALYSIS

### 2.1 The Critical Violation

**Article 5 Requirement:**

> "The certificate shall be in the form of a numbered checklist and shall include... the **surname** and **position** or title of the signing official"

**Observed Violation:**

- Field 2: "semnatura indescifrabila" (present)
- **Field 3: EMPTY - NO SURNAME/POSITION** (MISSING)
- Field 7: Digital signature present

**Legal Consequence:**
An apostille without the surname and position of the signing official certifies NOTHING. It is **VOID** under international law.
